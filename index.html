Who We Are

A School Built on Hope, Rooted in Community

Formerly known as Tendercare Motorstolic School Egypt, Otusile School has grown into one of Cairo's most compassionate and rigorous learning institutions. From our campus in Ain Shams, we serve students from across West, Central, and North Africa — with special outreach to children from conflict-affected nations including Sudan, South Sudan, and Eritrea.

We believe that displacement should never interrupt a child's right to quality education. Through partnerships with UNICEF, the United Nations, Catholic Relief Services, and other humanitarian organisations, we ensure that every learner — regardless of circumstance — receives the education they deserve.

Our Journey

From Tendercare to Otusile

What began as Tendercare Motorstolic School Egypt — a small, faith-rooted institution serving a handful of African families in Cairo — has transformed over 15 years into Otusile School: a thriving, multicultural academic community recognised by humanitarian organisations and respected across the African diaspora in Egypt.

Located in the vibrant district of Ain Shams, our school takes its name as a symbol of legacy and forward motion. We serve students from West Africa (Nigeria, Ghana, Senegal, Cameroon), Central Africa (DRC, Congo), North Africa, and — most critically — children displaced by conflict in Sudan, South Sudan, Eritrea, and neighbouring war-torn regions.

Our ethos is simple: no child's education should be interrupted by circumstance. Whether a student arrives with full academic records or arrives with nothing but courage, Otusile School opens its doors.

Est.

Founded as Tendercare Motorstolic School

The school is established in Ain Shams, Cairo, to serve the growing African community in Egypt, initially operating with a small cohort of West African students.

+3 Yrs

First Humanitarian Partnership

The school partners with Catholic Relief Services to begin accepting students displaced by conflict in Sudan and South Sudan, introducing dedicated pastoral support.

+7 Yrs

Cambridge Curriculum Adopted

Otusile introduces the Cambridge International curriculum alongside existing national frameworks, opening pathways to global university entry for graduates.

+10 Yrs

UNICEF & UN Collaboration

Formal collaboration with UNICEF and the United Nations brings expanded resources, feeding programmes, psychosocial support, and school materials for vulnerable children.

Today

Rebranded as Otusile School

The school completes its rebrand to Otusile School, marking a new chapter of growth, expanded enrolment, and deepened community impact across Cairo and beyond.

Academic Framework

Our Blended Curriculum

We operate a unique tri-curriculum model — the only school in Cairo offering all three simultaneously. This ensures every student can transition seamlessly and access globally recognised qualifications.

Sudan National Curriculum

Aligned with the Sudanese Ministry of Education. Taught in Arabic and English, covering Mathematics, Sciences, Social Studies, Islamic Studies (optional), and Languages. Ensures academic continuity for Sudanese students.

South Sudan Curriculum

Based on the Republic of South Sudan Ministry of General Education framework. Includes English as the medium of instruction, with mother-tongue support. Prioritises literacy, numeracy, and life skills.

Cambridge International

Cambridge IGCSE and Cambridge Primary programmes recognised in 160+ countries. Subjects include English, Mathematics, Sciences, Humanities, and ICT — preparing students for tertiary education worldwide.

Collaboration & Support

Our Humanitarian Partners

We are proud to work alongside some of the world's leading organisations committed to education, child welfare, and humanitarian relief.

UNICEF Egypt

UNICEF supports our school's feeding programme, provides learning materials, and funds psychosocial support for traumatised children from conflict zones.

United Nations (UNHCR)

Through UNHCR, refugee and asylum-seeking students access registration, documentation support, and educational continuity services.

Catholic Relief Services

CRS partners with us on community outreach, scholarships for the most vulnerable students, and integration programmes for newly arrived families.

International Rescue Committee (IRC)

IRC collaborates on emergency education responses, teacher training, and safe learning environment standards for displaced learners.

Community Churches & Diaspora Groups

Local faith communities and African diaspora associations contribute to extracurricular enrichment, cultural events, and family support networks.

Cambridge Assessment International

As an accredited Cambridge centre, we maintain the academic rigour and global recognition that our students deserve for their qualifications.

The Heart Behind
Otusile School

Meet the dedicated educator whose vision, compassion, and 15+ years of service have shaped a school that changes lives.

Ms. Esther Ediri Dolor
Founder & Proprietor
Proprietor & Founder

Ms. Esther Ediri Dolor

15+ Years in Education Educationist Community Leader Humanitarian Advocate Curriculum Specialist

Ms. Esther Ediri Dolor is a distinguished educationist with over 15 years of professional experience spanning teaching, school administration, curriculum development, and community advocacy. Born in West Africa and deeply connected to the African diaspora experience, Ms. Dolor founded Otusile School — originally as Tendercare Motorstolic School Egypt — with a single, powerful conviction: that every African child in Cairo deserves more than survival. They deserve excellence.

Her approach to education is holistic and deeply humane. She recognised early that displacement and conflict leave not just physical but psychological scars on children, and has consistently led the school's pastoral care and emotional wellbeing programmes alongside rigorous academics. Under her stewardship, Otusile School forged critical partnerships with UNICEF, the United Nations, Catholic Relief Services, and other organisations.

Ms. Dolor is also a respected voice in Cairo's African diaspora community, regularly advocating at forums on refugee education, child rights, and inclusive schooling. She is known for her open-door approach — to students, parents, teachers, and partners alike.

"My greatest achievement is not any certificate or award — it is looking into the eyes of a child who once had nothing, and seeing confidence, ambition, and joy. That is why we do this work."
